Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

Activity 1: Choosing the right chart

Summary:

  • Review the main objects you can use in Qlik Sense.
  • Deep into the different types of presentation that exist and which chart fits into each of them.
  • Think about visualization pitfalls and suggest alternative approaches.

Module: Creating visual analytics

Qlik Sense app: What chart to use

Challenge

  • Review the app and gain new insights on how to create useful visual analytics for users.

  • Import the app to your Personal space.

  • Analyze the charts, their different components such us the dimensions and measures.

  • Be able to think of new ideas to express what the author of the chart wants.

    • Qlik Sense Client-Managed: If you are working with the Skytap environment, the app will be available in the stream for this module.

    Qlik Sense Desktop: Place the .qvf file on the path Documents\Qlik\Sense\Apps. Then the app will appear on the Qlik Sense Desktop hub.

Hints

  • Go through the different app sheets.
  • If the sheet is public and you want to see the structure of the chart, click on Duplicate, on the right top of the sheet. Then you will be able to edit the sheet and explore the object configurations.
    • Note: As you are still new to many of these concepts, please don't hesitate to reach out for more guidance (Help site, instructor, Qlik Community).
    Step-by-step guide
    1. Go to the sheet "How do you decide which visualization to use?" and discuss the variety of analysis we can look for when using a chart in Qlik Sense.
    2. Continue to the next sheet "Qlik Sense chart..." and review the main charts you can find in the Assets panel of your apps.
    3. From the "Encoding data" to "Meaningful color" sheet, you will see different examples of options you can use when working with Qlik to represent data, highlight values, use gradients or colors.
    4. Go to the "Comparison" sheet. From that one to "Distribution and correlation" sheet, you must explore the objects that help to express each type of presentation. For instance, a line chart is really good at expressing composition over time.
    5. Then, analyze the "Visualization pitfalls 1" sheet, and try to answer these questions:
      • What is the difference between the two bar charts on the top left? What configuration was used to show exaggeration?
      • In the line chart below, why isn't the line chart the best option for this representation? Which chart would you use instead?
      • What can be done in the pie chart on the top to get a better visualization of the data?
      • Why is the bar chart on the bottom right called a "rainbow"? How can this visualization be improved?
    6. Finally, analyze the "Visualization pitfalls 2" sheet, and think and discuss about these questions:
      • Do you have some ideas to reduce the number of countries shown in the line chart and improve the user experience?
      • What is wrong with the bar chart? How can we take it to a next level?
      • What happens with the table? What would you make different here?
