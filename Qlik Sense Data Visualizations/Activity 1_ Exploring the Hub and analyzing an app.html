Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

Activity 1: Exploring the Hub and analyzing an app

Summary:

  • Gain familiarity with the Qlik hub.
  • Open an app, identify the parts or views in the app, and navigate through them.
  • Make simple selections and searches, apply bookmarks, and play stories.

Module: Overview, selecting and searching data

Qlik Sense app: ABC Sick Leave

Challenge

  • Familiarize yourself with the Qlik Sense hub, check the options to understand the platform you are using.
  • Import or copy and move the necessary Qlik Sense apps for this module into your Personal space.
  • Navigate through the apps and review the information related to each app.
  • Search for the Sick Leave app, open it, and identify components in the app (sheets, bookmarks, stories).
  • Analyze the data by making selections and be prepared to explain the following using existing visualizations and bookmarks:
    • Sick leave ratio for male - female groups in different regions and years (Example: China 2022, China 2023, etc.)
    • Correlation between sick leave days and overtime hours
  • Explore other selections and searches:
    • Compare Sick leave and Overtime hours for 2023 vs. China 2023 vs. USA 2023 using the Selection states sheets.
    • Find all the information about an employee (Ricardo Gucci) in the Sick Leave sheet using the Global search.

Qlik Sense Client-Managed: If you are working with the Skytap environment, the app or apps will be available in the stream for this module. Right-click on the app to duplicate them to move them to your Work area or personal area.

Qlik Sense Desktop: Place the .qvf file on the path Documents\Qlik\Sense\Apps. Then the app will appear on the Qlik Sense Desktop hub.

Hints

  • Visualizations in an app can be used to make selections. You can select one or more bars in the bar chart to reduce the data and make selections.
Step-by-step guide
  1. Discuss which Qlik Sense platform are you going to use for this activity.
  2. Navigate to the Qlik Sense hub and explore the options and navigation menus. Find the Help link as one of the options under the navigation menu in the top right area. Go to the Resource Center (between the Add new button and Notifications icon) and check out the suggested resources depending on your user type.
    3. Qlik Sense Client-Managed: When selecting the About option you can see the version of the product you are using. In Qlik Sense Cloud editions, you are working with the latest version available.
  3. Import or duplicate the apps into your Personal area, if needed:
    1. To import the apps, select Add new > Upload app option.
    2. To duplicate apps, click on the app and select Duplicate. Then move them to your personal area.

    Follow your instructor in this process to populate your Hub with the apps.

    Qlik Sense Desktop: Locate the app or apps on the local folder Documents\Qlik\Sense\Apps.
    Qlik Sense on Windows / Qlik Sense Desktop: Right click over the app icon to duplicate it.
  4. In the hub, hover over each app and see the related information. Then search for Sick Leave to filter the available apps.
  5. Open the ABC Sick Leave app. Explore in the App overview to see how many sheets, bookmarks, and stories are in this app. You can also check other views and areas in the app, like the Data model viewer, under the Prepare section, to see the tables, fields, and data associations.
  6. Navigate to the Sick Leave sheet, and analyze the visualizations. What is the Male-Female ratio of sick days?
  7. Make a selection by clicking on China in the bar chart. Confirm your selection by clicking on the green check mark icon. All the visualizations update with this selection because the data is associated, and the selection appears in the selections bar.

    The pie chart shows the male-female ratio of sick leave for China. You can change the dimension using the drop down arrow next to Gender.

  8. Click the bookmarks > button and select China 2022. Notice how the current selection changed. Review the pie chart values again.
  9. Using the selection bar at the top change the selected year to 2023 and click the> bookmarks button, then select Create new bookmark, to save the current selection (China and 2023) as a new personal bookmark.
  10. Click Clear all selections : and review the charts (combo chart and scatter plot) at the bottom of the sheet that show a correlation between sick leave days and overtime hours.
  11. Navigate to Storytelling and play the story called Sick Days and Overtime analysis.
  12. Go back to the Analyze view and navigate to the sheet called Selection states. In this sheet we identify three sets (Default state, State1 and State2). Each set of charts reflect only the selections in the corresponding state they are in. The bar chart on the right combines all the three states to compare them in the same chart. This allows you make comparisons easily.

    Select the following:

    1. Year 2023 in the Default state
    2. Year 2023 and China in the State1
    3. Year 2023 and USA in the State2

    Notice how the different selections are reflected in the selection bar at the top.

  13. Navigate to the previous sheet, clear all selections, and use the Smart search (Global search) icon at the top to type Gucci in the search box. Select Ricardo Gucci. Type 2023 and select that value on Year. Review all the info available in the sheet: Ricardo Gucci is a male employee that works for the Nice office (France) as Account Manager OEM in the Sales team. In 2023 he had 3 sick days and worked 22 overtime hours.
