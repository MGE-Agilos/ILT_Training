Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

(Optional) Activity 5: Designing a sheet with simplified authoring

Summary:

  • Explore simplified authoring experience while building a sheet.
  • Work with charts and explore their properties, and learn how to use filters.

Module: Creating visual analytics

Qlik Sense app: ABC Sales Analysis v1

Challenge

Create a new sheet called Sales Team Analysis in the ABC Sales Analysis v1 app, to analyze the performance of the Sales people around the world.

Then add the following information:

  • Design a Sales by Sales person analysis using the Autochart feature.

  • Show Sales team details: Sales person, JobTitle, Region, Country, City, Sales, Sales CYTD-LYTD, Discount, Margin.

Note: Measures and data shown depend on today's date. If you reload your app data, your results might differ from the solution image results.

Hints

  • The purpose of this activity is to gain some experience with the Simplified Authoring experience. This feature is only available in QS Cloud. If you are working with the other Qlik Sense version, you can develop this sheet in Advanced options mode, or go to the next module of the course.
  • In case you do not want to use Autochart, just select on of the objects available in the Visualization properties section.
  • From Presentation properties, Font family can be changed for all labels.
  • Use the Data table to check existing values in fields you want to be filtered on visualizations.
Step-by-step guide
  1. In the ABC Sales Analysis v1 app, create a new sheet with the title Sales Team Analysis and open it in edit mode. Be sure your are seeing the Simplified Authoring options.

    2. Sales by Sales person (Bar chart)

  2. Drag EmpName from the Fields pane on the left, and drop it in the Data properties > Drop field to use or for a suggestion, over the option Drop field to use it as a dimension. Then click over EmpName and type Sales person in Label.
  3. To have a better idea about the employees you are showing on this object, have a look at the Data table at the bottom. Select employees as Source table. Look at the Team column: you are displaying employees from all departments in your object, but you want to show only those from Sales Team.
  4. Look for the Team field on the left. Drag and drop into the Filters section and check Sales. See that the data in the object we are building is automatically filtered.
  5. Add Sales from the Master items pane. Drag and drop into the object, where says Drop master item to use as a measure. Now the table becomes a bar chart.
  6. Go to Labels under Presentation properties. Type Sales by Sales person in Title. Choose Arial in Font, and check Hide dimension title and Hide measure title.
  7. Click Color under Presentation properties, switch off Auto and select Single color and light blue (#b6d7ea).
  8. Click Styling under Presentation properties, choose Small as Outline with and set Bar width to 0.45.

    9. Sales team details (Table)

  9. Click on the button Add item below the bar chart.
  10. From Visualization properties, select Table .
  11. Using the Fields and Master items panel, add the following dimensions and measures:
    • Sales person as a dimension (add it from Master items)
    • JobTitle as a dimension
    • Region as a dimension
    • Country as a dimension
    • City as a dimension
    • Sales as a measure
    • Sales CYTD-LYTD as a measure
    • Discount as a measure
    • Margin as a measure
  12. Look for the Team field on the left. Drag and drop it into the Filters section and check Sales.
  13. Apply a proper number format to the measures by clicking on Sales, and selecting Number as Number formatting (1,000). Repeat this process for Sales CYTD-LYTD and Margin measures.
