Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

Activity 2: Creating objects with Insight Advisor

Summary:

  • Explore and work with Qlik Sense visualizations.
  • Use the Insight Advisor and the chart suggestions wizard to generate new visualizations in the app.
  • Create new visualizations using the Assets panel.

Module: Creating visual analytics

Qlik Sense app: ABC Sales Analysis v1

Challenge

The ABC company is developing a Qlik Sense app called ABC Sales Analysis v1 to measure sales results.

Include some visualizations in the app to answer the following business questions:

  • What is the division ranking based on sales amounts and the comparison of it with the Margin% amount?
    • The business users would like to see this in a combo chart, as the measures do not share the same units or scale.
    • Use the Division master dimension and Sales and Margin% measures created in the app to create this chart.
  • How is the sales trend over time (yearly or monthly)? And how is every year/month sales amount compare with the average across all years/ months?
    • Use the existing Sales trend master visualization.
    • Explore the properties of the existing master visualization.
  • Include an Overview of the Product, Sales, Country, Margin and UnitCost details in one visualization.
    • Explore how Qlik Sense generates the most suitable visualization based on the data displayed using the Chart suggestion wizard.
    • Use the Assets panel to drag and drop these dimensions, measures, and fields in the specified order.

  • Explore other ways to answer business questions in analysis, using the Qlik Insight Advisor. Search in the data to find out the following information and include the most relevant insights in the app:

    • Sales and Margin distribution by product in 2022
    • Show customer sales by country in Iberia
    • Show customer sales by country globally
  • Use the Insight Advisor Analysis types to include a composition of sales by Sales type and Category.

Hints

  • There are different ways of creating visualizations in Qlik Sense:
    • Select specific visualizations from the Charts menu, in the Assets panel, to create or convert visualizations into a specific chart.
    • Drag and drop Master items or Fields from the Assets panel onto the sheet. The Chart suggestion wizard displays and recommends the most suitable visual representation based on the data.
    • Use existing master visualizations stored in the app by dragging and dropping them from the the Assets panel > Master items > Visualizations menu.
    • In analysis mode, you can use the Insight Advisor (at the top bar) to perform natural language searches in the data and include the relevant visual results in the different sheets of the app, or to use the Analysis types to create faster a chart based on your needs.
  • You can explore other Chart suggestions from the properties panel of each object. This feature can also be turned off to explore advanced properties in each object.
Step-by-step guide
  1. Open the ABC Sales Analysis v1 app and create a new sheet called Sales Overview.

    2. Creating the division ranking using a Combo chart

    Note: You may need to use the Advanced options toggle to access the Assets panel in Edit mode.

  2. Open the new sheet and use edit mode to modify the sheet by selecting the @ button.
  3. From the Assets panel, under the Charts menu, drag and drop the Combo chart onto the sheet.
  4. Add a title by typing in the chart title, Sales and Margin% by Division.
  5. In the new chart, click Add dimension, and type division in the search input box. Then select the Division field under Dimensions.
  6. Click Add measure and select Sales from the Measures section of the pop up menu. The visualization displays showing the sales amounts as bars.
  7. Select the visualization, then from the Properties panel on the right, under the Data section, add a new measure using the Add button under Height of line, and select Margin%.
  8. In the properties panel, under Data, after expanding the Margin% section, click the More properties button. Under Presentation, select Marker. Then, from the Dimensions section, expand the Division section and clear Include null values.
  9. In the properties panel, under Sorting, use the drag bars to move Sales at the top.
  10. In the properties panel, under Presentation, choose Horizontal.

    11. Adding a Sales trend chart

  11. From the Assets panel, under the Master items > Visualizations menu, drag and drop the Sales trend master visualization onto an empty space in the sheet. A line chart displaying the sales over time is added to the sheet.
  12. Select the chart to review its properties. Note that this is a Linked visualization.
    13. Note: Linked visualizations added to the sheet can be changed or edited by clicking Edit in the properties panel but the changes will be saved to the master visualization.

    Overview of Product, Sales, Country, Margin, and UnitCost details

  13. From the Assets panel, under the Master items > Dimensions menu, search then drag and drop the Product dimension onto an empty space in the sheet. A table with the product values is shown.
  14. From the Assets panel, under the Master items > Measures menu, drag and drop the Sales measure onto the Product table. Now the visualization is converted to a bar chart.
  15. From the Assets panel, under the Master items > Dimensions menu, drag and drop the Customer dimension onto the bar chart in the sheet. Qlik Sense adapts the chart to a more suitable representation, a distribution plot.
  16. From the Assets panel, under the Master items > Measures menu, drag and drop the Margin measure onto the visualization. Now the visualization is converted to a scatter plot.
  17. From the Assets panel, under the Fields menu, drag and drop the UnitCost field onto the visualization. Notice how the bubble sizes changed based on this field. With the visualization selected, review in the properties panel how the basic aggregation is being applied to this field.
  18. In the properties panel, select the Auto Scatter plot section at the top, to review other visual suggestions for this data. Then select Table under Recommended charts.
  19. Double click in a corner of the table to expand the table to cover all the empty space in the sheet.
  20. From the Table properties panel, format the measures to money format.
  21. Stop editing the sheet, and in analysis mode adjust the column's width in the table. Then in edit mode, select the table and apply this change using the Apply button in the Properties panel.

    22. Creating visualizations based on Insights

  22. Go to Insight Advisor. Then enter your first query in the input box: "Display the Sales and Margin distribution by product in 2022".
  23. Review the matching results and use the Add to sheet button to include the scatter plot in the Sales Overview sheet.
  24. Clear your first search and enter a new search in the input box: "Show me customer sales by country in Iberia". Inspect the results and review the filter that is applied. Note Iberia is a term created in the Vocabulary of the app.
  25. Under Additional results, select the Distribution of Sales for Country map, clear now the filters for Country, and add it to the Sales Overview sheet. Then, once added, use the link at the bottom right of the visualization to navigate to the sheet.
  26. Readjust and relocate the charts as shown in the solution image. Then click Edit sheet to review the sheet and analyze it.

    27. Qlik Sense Client-Managed: If you are working with the Skytap environment, click Done editing to return to the navigation mode.

    Adding a breakdown analysis by Sales type and Category

  27. Go to Insight Advisor. Pick Breakdown from analysis types.
  28. Select Sales as measure, and Sales type and CategoryName as dimensions.
  29. Review the matching results and use the Add to sheet button to include the treemap in the Sales Overview sheet. Place it between the scatter plot and the table.
  30. In the properties panel, under Colors and legend deactivate Auto and select By measure.

Solution

Click on the Solution image to open as a pop-up, or right-click on the image for more options. To close, click on the image again.
