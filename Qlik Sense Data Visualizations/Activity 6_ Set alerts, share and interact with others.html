Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

Activity 6: Set alerts, share and interact with others

Summary:

  • Understand how to set conditions in the app to trigger alerts base on the data.
  • Publish or move the app to streams or spaces.
  • Learn how to create subscription reports based on visualizations.
  • Work with others using notes.

Module: Sharing insights, monitoring and finalizing the app

Qlik Sense app: ABC Sales Dashboard v2

Challenge

In the ABC Sales Dashboard v2 app, create an alert that gets trigger when Margin% is under 15% based on the current year for each division in America.

Make some of the sheets public in the app if you are working with Cloud editions of Qlik Sense and share it with others using any of these options:

  • Move the app to a shared space (in Qlik Cloud)
  • Publish the app to a managed space (in Qlik Cloud) or to a stream (in Qlik Client-Managed)

Qlik Sense on Windows: Notes and subscription reports are not supported.

Qlik Sense Desktop: This activity is not supported.

Bonus point:

Create a report about the ABC Sales and send it to your email.

Hints

  • When creating the alert, apply the Division dimension and the Americas bookmark to define the data set. Then compare the Margin% with the 0.15 value.
  • In Cloud editions of Qlik Sense, make sheets and stories public before publishing or moving the app to a space.
  •  You can create alerts based on measures in the app and apply specific dimensions and selections.
    • In Qlik Cloud, right-click on a visualization to set an alert. You can manage and monitor your alerts in the Qlik Cloud Management console. Note Data alerts must be enabled in the Settings section of the Management console.
    • In Qlik Client-Managed, use the Qlik Alerting extension to enable users to create alerts. You can create and manage system and data alerts from the Qlik Alerting portal.
  • You can subscribe to reports about visualizations and sheets in an app. Note: Subscriptions must be enabled in the Settings section of the Management console, under the Email sharing section. An email server must be also configured for users to receive reports by email.
  • You can create notes to collaborate with others and share your insights. Note: Notes must be enabled in the Settings section of the Management console, under Feature control section.
  • Reports can be created using a Qlik App Application Automation and the Qlik Reporting connector available in there.
Step-by-step guide
  1. In the ABC Sales Dashboard v2 app, open the ABC Dashboard sheet in edit mode. Make sure all selections are cleared.

Note: Steps differ between Qlik Client-Managed and Qlik Cloud. Follow the corresponding steps based on your environment.

Qlik Cloud

    Create alert

  1. Go to the Alerts section in the Qlik Cloud Hub. From here you can enable or disable alerts and manage them. Now you will create your own alert in the app.
  2. In the ABC Dashboard sheet, right-click on the Margin% KPI and create a new alert.
  3. Under Title, enter Margin% lower than 15%. Margin% is already added. Note, you could add additional measures.
  4. Then select Division in the Show by dimension drop-down and the Americas bookmark under Selections. Note how the Data preview displays a table with two rows.
  5. Click Next and set the condition selecting Margin %, Less than, and Value in the drop-downs. Then enter 0.15 in the Value input box. Note how the Preview alert section displays only the rows where the condition is met. If no rows meet the condition the alert is not triggered.
  6. Click Next and set the notification frequency to trigger the alert weekly. Then click Create.
  7. Note how the alert is displayed in the hub. Click on the notification message to see the details of the alert. In the Alert panel, you can review the alert details, view the history of the notifications for the alert, check recipients and navigate to the app.

    8. Publish the app or moving the app to a space

  8. In the App overview, under Sheets, right-click in some of the sheets under the My sheets area, then select Make Public.
  9. Repeat the same for Bookmarks and Stories in the app.
  10. In the Cloud hub, under Catalog > Personal area, click on the 3 dots button in the app you want to share and select Publish to share the app in a Managed space. Alternatively you can select Move if you want to share it in a Shared space.
  11. Select the Space where the app is going to be published or moved. When you publish the app you can change the app title, description, and add tags. You can only publish apps to a managed space. If you don't have managed spaces in your Cloud environment you can use the Move option instead and move the app to a shared space.
  12. Note, if you move the app to a shared space, the app is no longer in your personal area, while if you publish the app into a managed space, the app remains in your personal area as well for further development. Discuss other differences between managed and shared spaces. Your instructor will demonstrate how to manage user access to each space and the user rights available.

    13. Create subscription report

  13. The instructor will access the Cloud Management console and show you the Subscriptions section. From here the tenant administrator can enable or disable alerts and manage them. Now you will create your own subscription report in the app.
  14. In the ABC Dashboard sheet, right-click on the Top 10 selling products bar chart and create a new subscription, and leave Title as it is.
  15. Choose Weekly on Schedule options.
  16. Note that you could add other users as recipients of the report if you want. Then click Subscribe.
  17. Now the subscription is displayed in the hub. Click on the notification message to see the details. In the Subscriptions panel, you can review the details, the history of the reports for the subscription, check recipients and navigate to the app.

    18. Create note

  18. In the ABC Dashboard sheet, right-click on the Sales share pie chart and create a new note with the snapshot of the chart. Click on Create note with snapshot.
  19. Type "EMEA share is higher than Americas's" as Title.
  20. Write some text on the note. In case more users have access to the space where you have created the note (you can see the name of the space on the bottom of the Notes panel), you can type @ to mention them. They will receive a notification and will be able to participate on editing the note.

    21. Note: When a user is added to a space, whether directly as an individual user or indirectly as a member of a group, they will have Can view permissions for any notes created in the space.

  21. In case you want to modify permissions of other users over you note, click on icon.
  22. Then go to the Hub > Catalog, filter Types by Notes and check that the note is on the corresponding space.

    23. Bonus point:

  23. From the Hub, click on Add new > New automation.
  24. Select Reporting under the Categories section and choose the Send a simple multi-page report by email template. Hover over the template tile and click theUse template button. Note that a series of blocks are already placed into the automation scheme.
  25. Leave the Start block as it is.
  26. Click on Variable - recipients and, on the right panel, write the email where you want to receive the report in the second gray box under the Operations on recipients.
  27. Then, click on the Create report tile and select the app from the scroll bar. If you select do lookup, you will see the list of all the apps you have access to, and you can select it easily.
  28. Go to Send Mail > Connection. Click the button Connect your Mail(in case you already have a connection configured, you can use it or create a new one). We will use a Gmail account. In order to ensure that the report can be delivered correctly, you must create an App password in your Gmail account. Follow this instructions to get it.
  29.  Then, complete the connection configuration, that will look like this:
    • Host: smtp.gmail.com
    • Port: 587
    • Security: starttls
    • Sender_Email_Address: your Gmail address
    • Username: leave blank
    • Password: the app password that you have just generated
  30. Click Run on the top of the page. When finishes, you will receive the report in your email.
Qlik Client-Managed

    Create alert

  1. In a new Chrome browser tab, go to the Qlik Alerting portal: https://qse:4552/#/
  2. If credentials are required, enter Student as name and Training1 as password. From this panel you can monitor your alerts or create new ones.
  3. Under Admin > User management > Users, review the Email column set for the users. Here you can change the email address of your user (student). Click the 3 dots in the student row and select Edit. Then under Email ID, enter your personal email and click Save.
  4. Go back into the Alerts section to monitor the data alerts. You, as administrator, could create new alerts from here, but for this activity we will use the Qlik alerting extension to demonstrate how users can create their own alerts.
  5. Go back to the Qlik Sense hub, and in the app, edit the ABC dashboard sheet and make some free space in the sheet.
  6. From the Assets panel > Custom objects > Extensions, drag the Qlik Alerting extension onto the free space in the sheet.
  7. In the extension properties panel, under Qlik Alerting server, replace localhost with qse. Then under Style, turn the Show icon property to On. This new button allows users to create their data alerts.
  8. In analysis mode, click the Create alert button to trigger the alert creation wizard.
  9. Set an alert name: Margin% lower than 15% and click Next.
  10. From the Measures drop-down, select Margin%. Then click Add measure.
  11. From the Dimension (optional) select Division and click Next.
  12. In the conditions drop-down select Margin%, Less than (<), and Manual value. Then enter 0.15 in the Value input box and click Next.
  13. Under Selections / Bookmarks select the Americas bookmark, under Frequency select Only once, and for Channels select Email. Then click Create alert.
  14. A message is displayed to confirm the alert is created. Click Close.
  15. Navigate to the Qlik Alerting portal in the other browser tab, refresh the page and review the data alerts. You can trigger the new alert using the 3 dots button and selecting Trigger. Then select Confirm.
  16. Check your email inbox to see if you have an email based on the alert. If you don’t see the email, make sure you review your spam mail. Note this email is the one set for the student user you edited in step 3.

    17. Publishing the app to a stream

  17. Under Personal > Work area in the hub, right-click on the app and select Publish.
  18. Select the ABC Apps stream. You can leave the name as is or give a new name to the app. Note if the app already exists in the stream you also have the option to replace the existing one.
  19. Click Publish. Note how the app has been moved from your personal area to the ABC Apps stream.
  20. Select the stream and open the published app. Note all the sheets are public and you cannot edit them. You can still create new sheets or duplicate existing sheets in this app.
