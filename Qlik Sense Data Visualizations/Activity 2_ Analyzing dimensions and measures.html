Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

Activity 2: Analyzing dimensions and measures

Summary:

  • Identify different visualizations and the dimensions and measures in them.
  • Change visualizations using the alternative dimensions and measures and the Exploration menu.

Module: Overview, selecting and searching data

Qlik Sense app: Vintage movies

Challenge

Open the Vintage Movies app in a managed space or stream in the hub, navigate through the sheets, and analyze the visualizations.

Qlik Sense Desktop: Qlik Sense Desktop does not offer streams or managed/shared spaces. Locate the app in your personal space to work in this activity.

Qlik Sense Business: Managed spaces are not supported in this version but you can use shared spaces or your personal space for this activity.

  • What are the main dimensions and measures, and what visualizations are being used in this sheet?:
    • Dashboard sheet
    • Dimensions and Expressions (1) sheet

    Explore how you can change the visualizations without editing the sheet:

    • In the Movies and Director Analysis sheet, adjust the alternative dimensions and measures in the charts.
    • In the Movies across time sheet, use the Exploration menu to change how the bar chart looks.

    Hints

    • A dimension groups the data in the visualization and a measure is usually aggregated with a function.
    • When a visualization has reduced space, some of the options might not be visible. Hover over the chart and click s in the top right corner to work with the object in full screen mode; click E to return to normal view.
    • You can interact with select properties in some visualizations while in analysis mode. When the app is opened in analysis mode (not edit), use the Exploration menu Ú by right-clicking over the chart.
    Step-by-step guide
    1. Open the Vintage Movies app, then, from the App overview, navigate to the Dashboard sheet.
    2. Review the charts and visualizations in this sheet:
      • At the top we find three KPIs representing main measures for the analysis. The first KPI includes a secondary measure, too.
      • Three bar charts displayed with a horizontal layout show the distribution of the measures across some dimensions (decade, director, actor). Notice that when selecting a decade in the first bar chart, the dimension changes and drills into the years of that selected decade.
      • Additional information about the trend of the number of movies (line chart) and the share of the rating (donut or pie chart) can be found in the sheet.
    3. Navigate to the Dimensions and Expressions (1) sheet. Review the different type of charts and identify what measures and dimensions are being used in them. Expand the solution section below to check your answers:
      Solution
      • The first visualization is a combo chart that represents the number of movies and average ratings per decade:
        • Dimension: Decade
        • Measures: Number of movies (count) and Avg rating (average)
      • The second visualization is a gauge and represents the average rating over all the movies. There is no dimension in this chart.
        • Measure: Avg rating (average)
      • The third visualization is a pie chart that displays the share of movies between the ratings.
        • Dimension: Rating
        • Measure: Number of movies (count)
      • The fourth visualization is a line chart displaying a trend of the number of movies over time (by year) where each line represents a rating.
        • Dimensions: Year and Rating
        • Measure: Number of movies (count)
    4. Navigate to the Movies and Director Analysis sheet and interact with some of the charts. Note that you can expand them hovering over them and viewing them in Full screen mode (s).
    5. Use the Number of films and average rating over time chart to select one decade and see the yearly trend number of movies and ratings. In this case, a drill-down dimension is being used to display the hierarchy between decades and years in the decades. Also, in this chart, there are two Y-axes scales (left and right) as the two measures used in the chart have different scales.
    6. Use the drop down arrows next to the measures and dimension names in the axes of the visualizations to change the measures and dimensions picking other alternative ones that were preconfigured by the app author.
    7. Navigate to the previous sheet, Movies across time, and clear selections. Notice how the text at the top of the sheet is dynamic text and it calculates some expressions based on the current selection.
    8. Hover over the bar chart, select the More menu and open the Exploration menu. The chart expands to full screen and the Exploration menu becomes visible.
    9. In the Exploration menu, change the visualization properties:
      • In the Presentation section, change the orientation of the bars and the display from stack to grouped.
      • In the Sorting section, sort by Decade-Year ascending, then Rating ascending, and after them, Number of movies ascending.
      • In the Data section, select the alternative measure.
    10. You need to click Apply to save the changes. Note that you can use the Discard button to return the chart to the original state.

    Solution

    Click on the Solution image to open as a pop-up, or right-click on the image for more options. To close, click on the image again.
