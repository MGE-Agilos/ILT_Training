Create Visualizations with Qlik Sense

Activity 3: Create calendar measures

Summary:

  • Use the Data manager to prepare the tables for analysis by adding source filters, transforming fields, and creating new fields.
  • Familiarize with the Visual preparation (Data manager associations) to associate the tables and create the data model.

Module: Preparing data for analysis

Qlik Sense app: ABC Sales

Dependencies: Before working in this activity, make sure you complete Activity 1.

Challenge

In the ABC Sales app, create the following calendar measures using the specified names:

Calendar measure name Description

Sales YTD

Sum of Sales for the Year to Date (YTD) based on the OrderDate.

Sales CYTD

Sum of Sales for the Current Year to Date (CYTD) based on the OrderDate.
Sales LYTD

Sum of Sales for the Last Year to Date (LYTD) based on the OrderDate.

 

Bonus Point:

Include two additional master measures with YTD Flags:

Calendar measure name Description
Sales CYTD - LYTD Difference between the Sum of Sales for the CYTD and the LYTD.
Num Orders CYTD Count distinct OrderID for the Current Year to Date (CYTD) based on the OrderDate.

Hints

  • Calendar measures allow you to generate several master measures at once based on time ranges (yearly, quarterly, etc).
  • Calendar measures are created from the Fields section in the Assets panel. Right-click on the field you want to apply the expression, for instance, the Sales field, and select Create calendar measures.
  • You can reuse existing master measures to create a new one by right-clicking the measure and selecting Duplicate. Then you can Edit the duplicated measure, changing the name and expression.
Step-by-step guide
  1. In the ABC Sales app, open My new sheet in edit mode.

    2. Creating Sales YTD, Sales CYTD, and Sales LYTD

  2. In the Assets panel, expand the Fields list and right-click on the Sales field.
  3. Select Create calendar measures.
  4. Select OrderDate as Date field, Sum as the Aggregation function and Yearly as the Time range.
  5. Click Save to master items and close the window. This option generates, by default, three master measures.

  6. In the Assets panel, under é Master items, expand Measures and review the three new measures:

    • SUM Sales YTD - for the year to date time range based on today's date (applicable to all the years in the app)
    • SUM Sales YTD Current Year - for the year to date based on today's date (and for the current year based on today's year)
    • SUM Sales YTD Last Year - for the year to date based on today's date (and for the previous year based on today's year)

  7. Edit these master measures to change the Name in each of them to Sales YTD, Sales CYTD, Sales LYTD as in the table above.

    8. Bonus Point:

    Creating Sales CYTD - LYTD

  8. Right-click the Sales CYTD field and select Duplicate. A copy of the Sales CYTD master item is created.
  9. Edit the Sales CYTD (1) and rename it to Sales CYTD - LYTD.
  10. Change the Expression as follows and click Save:

    [Sales CYTD]-[Sales LYTD]

    Note: Existing master measures can also be used as part of other expressions by referencing them by their measure labels.

    11. Creating Num Orders CYTD

  11. Right-click the Sales CYTD and select Duplicate. A copy of the Sales CYTD master item is created.
  12. Edit the Sales CYTD (1) and rename it to Num Orders CYTD.
  13. Change the following in the Expression and click Save:
    1. Change the initial part of the expression "Sum(" with "Count(distinct"
    2. Change the final part of the expression "[Sales])" with "OrderID)"

      3. The final expression looks as follows:

      Count( Distinct { $ < [OrderDate.autoCalendar.InYTD]={1}, [OrderDate.autoCalendar.YearsAgo]={0} > } OrderID )
Note: The results of the YTD calculations depend on the current date (today).
